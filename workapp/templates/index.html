Welcome to Crawford Work Study Program



About the Program

The Crawford Work-Study program (WSP) department offers students practical experience and exposure to instill the values of labor dignity and create opportunities for earning income.


Objectives

The primary aim of the work-study program is not only to alleviate financial burdens but also to enable students to work while they learn. This helps them integrate and prepare more effectively into a changing society after graduation.

Crawford University runs a work-study program that provides limited employment opportunities for students to help reduce their financial burdens. A substantial amount of the university's budget is allocated to support this program.



Guidelines

  • Students are allowed to participate in this program once per academic session (either in the first semester or the second semester).
  • You are required to work eight times in a semester. You can complete these eight sessions within one month or spread them out, but ensure you work a total of eight times.
  • Students must work 2 hours per day, two days a week (i.e., 4 hours per week) for four weeks in a month. Any deviation from this schedule will result in deductions from your payment.
  • When choosing your placement, keep in mind that you might not get your preferred location due to certain circumstances (first come, first served).
  • You will need to send your daily log activities to your supervisor for approval. After completing the work activities, there will be a final submission to the director of the department/admin for review and payment
  • Payment will be calculated based on the hours and days the student has worked. Full payment for completing the program is ten thousand naira only (â‚¦10,000).


Eligibility: To register for the program, you must meet the following criteria:

  • Be a full-time undergraduate student.
  • Provide a passport photograph.
  • Have at least a second-class lower division standing.
  • Obtain a recommendation from your Head of Department (HOD) with a stamp.
  • Upload your student ID card.
  • Have an active email address and phone number.
